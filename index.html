The Sacred Rules

01

Thou Shalt Not Snitch

This is the cornerstone. The bedrock. The very foundation upon which sister-hood is built. You saw nothing. You know nothing. Whatever she was doing on Friday night is between her, God, and whoever she was texting. Your role is to provide a convincing alibi and ask zero follow-up questions.

* This rule does not extend to situations involving genuine danger, fire, or the last slice of pizza.

Borrow Clothes, But Make It Fashion

A good sister borrows clothes without asking because asking creates a paper trail, and a paper trail creates accountability, and accountability is simply not the vibe. Return items within a reasonable timeframe ‚Äî "reasonable" meaning before she notices, or never, whichever comes first. If caught, compliment her taste immediately.

Always Take Her Side (In Public)

In the presence of outsiders, parents, or anyone with the power to ground either of you, your sister is 100% correct and you fully support her. Whatever story she is telling, you witnessed it. It happened exactly that way. The other person is clearly terrible. At home, however, you are free to inform her that she is being ridiculous and also kind of dramatic.

Deliver the Truth (Brutally)

Friends will lie to you. A sister will look you dead in the eyes and say "that haircut is not working for you, babe." This is not cruelty. This is a service. The world needs more people willing to say "you cannot pull off that color" before you leave the house. A good sister is that person, every single time, without apology.

Master the Shared Look

The Shared Look is a silent, telepathic exchange that communicates entire paragraphs in under half a second. A slight raise of the eyebrow says "can you BELIEVE what just happened." A slow blink says "I am so tired of this family." A barely-suppressed smirk says "we will be laughing about this in the car." Practice until fluent. This is your secret language.

* Outsiders may not learn The Look. It is inherited, not taught.

Provide Mandatory Snack Tax

If you are making food, you are making food for two. This is not a question. This is gravity.
